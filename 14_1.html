About BCCI




The Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) is India's national governing body for cricket, responsible for managing the sport domestically and internationally, selecting teams, and running events like the IPL. It's the world's wealthiest cricket board, known for its significant revenue and influence, managing both men's and women's cricket, overseeing player contracts, and operating the National Cricket Academy (NCA). Formed in 1928, it governs all cricket in India, from grassroots to the national team, ensuring fair play and integrity. Key Functions & Responsibilities: Governance: Develops and promotes cricket across India. Team Management: Selects players, coaches, umpires, and manages national teams (men's & women's). Tournament Organization: Conducts domestic tournaments (like the Ranji Trophy) and international matches. Financial Powerhouse: Manages huge revenues from broadcasting, sponsorships, and the IPL, making it the richest board globally. Player Welfare: Oversees player contracts, salaries (graded system), and provides scholarships. Infrastructure: Runs the National Cricket Academy (NCA) for player development. Key Facts: Full Form: Board of Control for Cricket in India. Founded: 1928. Headquarters: Cricket Centre, Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai. Influence: A key part of the "Big Three" in world cricket. In essence, the BCCI is the powerful entity that controls the flow, development, and financial backbone of Indian cricket, from grassroots to the international stage.
